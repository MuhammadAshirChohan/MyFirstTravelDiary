Murree

Murree, often called the "Queen of the Hills," is a popular hill station in the Punjab province of Pakistan, known for its stunning natural beauty and cool climate. Situated in the Pir Panjal Range of the Himalayas, it offers breathtaking scenery, lush green hills, and a refreshing escape from the heat of the plains. Founded by the British as a sanatorium and summer capital, Murree retains colonial-era architecture and a charming atmosphere. Today, it's a favorite destination for tourists seeking both relaxation and adventure, with attractions like Mall Road, Kashmir Point, and the Patriata (New Murree) chairlift.

Hunza Valley

Hunza Valley, often called "Heaven on Earth," is a breathtaking region in northern Pakistan known for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Nestled in the Karakoram Range, it boasts towering peaks like Rakaposhi and Ultar Sar, along with picturesque landscapes and vibrant green valleys. Beyond its scenic splendor, Hunza offers a unique cultural experience with ancient forts like Baltit Fort and Altit Fort, traditional music and dance, and warm, hospitable locals. It's a haven for adventure tourism, with opportunities for trekking, mountaineering, and exploring glaciers like the Passu Glacier.

Rawal Pindi

Rawalpindi, commonly known as Pindi, is a vibrant city in Punjab, Pakistan, and is known as one of the "twin cities" alongside Islamabad. It's a major commercial and industrial hub, and was the capital of Pakistan from 1959 to 1969 while Islamabad was under construction. Located on the Potohar Plateau, Rawalpindi boasts a rich history, including its ancient Buddhist heritage, and its role as a significant British garrison town. Today, it's a bustling metropolis with a diverse population and strong social and economic ties to its neighboring capital.
